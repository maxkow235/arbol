How ARBOL Works

The farmer and the investor each pay ether into a smart contract and receive a Weather Immunity Token (WIT)

The WIT is a ERC 721 token tradeable in secondary markets

If data shows rainfall shortfall, the farmer’s WIT gets the full escrow payment of ether or else it goes to the investor

ETHANOL & BIODIESEL

Ethanol and biodiesel are petroleum substitutes made from agricultural products. The most familiar of these is corn ethanol in the US and sugar ethanol in Brazil, both of which are blended into gasoline. Similar to livestock operators, biofuel plants are very sensitive to raw materials costs, and during the 2012 drought, many corn ethanol plants faced huge losses or were forced to shut down. These plants can use ARBOL to generate a payment in the case of local drought, which would help protect them against spiking materials costs. ​

For US corn based plants, a July-August growing season drought WIT is ideal. For a Brazil sugar ethanol plant, low rainfall for sugar during the October - March period is the key risk to sugarcane growth. For sugar based plants, the other risk is too much rainfall during harvest season from April to October, which cuts the sugar content of the plant. This risk can also be hedged easily on ARBOL.
